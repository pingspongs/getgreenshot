Roman Polanski and Bradley Cooper headline the Venice Film Festival on Saturday, though controversy and a Hollywood strike mean neither is attending.<\/p>\n

Still technically a fugitive from US justice over a child sex conviction in the 1970s, the 90-year-old Polanski will be absent for the premiere of \u2018The Palace\u2019, his farce set in a Swiss hotel that could be the legendary director’s last film.<\/p>\n

An ongoing actors strike in Hollywood means Cooper and Carey Mulligan will also be missing from the launch of their movie \u2018Maestro\u2019 about legendary conductor and composer Leonard Bernstein and his wife.<\/p>\n

Though Cooper is also director, he chose to stay home in solidarity with the strikers, foregoing the splashy red carpet launch he gave his previous hit, \u2018A Star is Born\u2019, in Venice.<\/p>\n

There has been some internet-driven controversy over Cooper’s decision to wear a large prosthetic nose — seen by some as perpetuating stereotypes about Jews — though Bernstein’s family defended the film and said the composer would not have minded and confirmed he had a “nice, big nose”<\/p>\n

‘Full of criminals’
\nSince the MeToo movement, Polanski’s conviction for child sex in the 1970s has overshadowed his work and made him a pariah in Hollywood.<\/p>\n

Still revered for classics like \u2018Chinatown\u2019, \u2018Rosemary’s Baby\u2019 and \u2018The Pianist\u2019, his selection in the out-of-competition section at Venice has been widely criticised by feminist groups and others.<\/p>\n

His new film stars Mickey Rourke and John Cleese as guests in a fancy hotel for the turn of the millennium.<\/p>\n

For decades, Polanski’s conviction was largely overlooked, particularly in Europe where he continued to work and win awards, right up to his last film, the France-based \u2018An Officer and a Spy\u2019, which won the Jury prize in Venice in 2019.<\/p>\n

But anger over that film’s success at France’s Cesar Awards proved a rallying point for MeToo anger in the country, which had been a haven for the director for years, especially as fresh assault allegations against Polanski came to light.<\/p>\n

Venice festival director Alberto Barbera defended the decision to include him, telling AFP: “The history of art is full of artists who were criminals, and we nonetheless continue to admire their work.”<\/p>\n

\u2018Maestro\u2019 is among 23 films competing for the Golden Lion in Venice, to be decided on 9 September.<\/p>\n

A towering figure of 20th-century classical music, Bernstein assured his place in popular history by composing the Broadway smash musical \u2018West Side Story\u2019. The Venice competition has one frontrunner so far after rave reviews for \u2018Poor Things\u2019 on Friday.<\/p>\n

It saw Emma Stone as a sexually voracious reanimated corpse in a darkly comic, and strongly feminist, reimagining of Frankenstein. In response to the low-fat trend of the 1990s, numerous food businesses removed saturated fats from their products, only to replace them with sugars to retain palatability.<\/p>\n

Unfortunately, the new products were no healthier than the originals, and the average individual now consumes far too much saturated fat.<\/p>\n
